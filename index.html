RTLS — Nanotag IR & The Sensing Foundation

The Nanotag IR is Kontakt.io's patient wristband tag — combining BLE room-level tracking with infrared confirmation for sub-second room-exit detection. It goes on a separate wristband from the patient ID band (the ID band can never be removed for safety; the nanotag is not MRI-safe). When it exits a room, every downstream workflow fires automatically.

The Automated Workflow — Step by Step
From Epic discharge to new patient occupying the room — with zero manual coordination steps.
1

Attending Writes Discharge Order — Clinical Process Begins

The physician signs the discharge order in Epic, starting the clinical discharge process. The bedside nurse begins discharge education: medications, follow-up appointments, and instructions. This typically takes 1–2 hours. During this window, the patient is preparing to leave — but the room still appears occupied in the EHR because nurse documentation is incomplete. This is where the 90-minute bottleneck lives in the status quo.

2

RTLS Detects Patient Departure — The Trigger That Eliminates the Bottleneck

When the patient's Nanotag IR wristband exits the room, Kontakt.io detects physical departure within seconds — without waiting for nurse documentation. For Epic customers, this RTLS data automatically triggers Epic's built-in RTLS Interface to mark the room soiled and alert EVS. This is Stage 1: the primary value driver, saving up to 75 of the 90 minutes per discharge by removing the documentation dependency entirely. Nurses still complete documentation, but it's no longer a bottleneck. Staff can also press the BLE room button (blue = standard clean, red = biohazard) as a manual override.

3

EVS Receives Automated Work Request

The EVS team gets an immediate, room-specific notification on their mobile device: which room, what type of clean, and priority level. No phone call. No rounding to find dirty rooms. No discovery delay. EVS gets to work while the patient is still in the elevator.

4

Parallel Transport Dispatch — Stage 2 (The Extra 15 Minutes)

When EVS is approximately 30 minutes into a predictable 45-minute terminal clean, the system knows transport needs ~30 minutes to arrive. Stage 2 dispatches transport now — in parallel with cleaning — so the next patient is en route as the room clears. This eliminates the "wait for clean confirmation, then call transport" sequence. Stage 2 saves an additional ~15 minutes per discharge on top of Stage 1. Seller note: Stage 2 is sold but rarely activated in practice, since Stage 1 via Epic RTLS Interface already delivers the primary 90-minute benefit.

5

Room Status Updates — EHR and Dashboard Simultaneously

When EVS marks the room clean, status updates simultaneously in Epic and in the Kontakt.io dashboard. Bed Management, patient placement, charge nurses, and Command Center all see the bed become available the instant it is — not 60 minutes later. No one has to call anyone to find out.

6

Patient Assigned — Room Occupied — Data Captured

With real-time bed visibility, Bed Management assigns the next patient immediately. The new patient is transported to a verified-ready room. Analytics capture every timestamp — vacancy detected, EVS alert sent, cleaning complete, patient assigned — giving leadership a full audit trail and continuous improvement baseline.
